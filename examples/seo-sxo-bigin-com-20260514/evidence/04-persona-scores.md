# 04 — Persona scores: bigin.com vs "best CRM for small business" SERP

> Persona rubric: `references/persona-rubrics.md` in the skill folder. 4 personas, 0–10 each. Intent profile = commercial-investigation; persona weights: Skimmer 35%, Researcher 30%, Buyer 25%, Validator 10%.
> Note: persona scores reflect a structural assessment of bigin.com vs the dominant SERP pattern. The base composite is then capped at 35 by the "page-type mismatch" rule per the skill's tip — that's why the final score is 32 not 35.

## Skimmer (35% weight) — score 4/10

**What the Skimmer needs (per rubric):**
- See the top 3-5 options within 5 seconds of landing.
- Visual hierarchy that gives a "winners list."
- Comparison table or bullet ranking above the fold.

**What bigin.com delivers:**
- Hero: "Your business needs a simple CRM" — brand-centric, not comparative.
- No top-5 list, no comparison table, no winners ranking.
- Carousel of customer quotes — slow to skim.

**Gap:** Skimmer arrives expecting a "Top 10" page. Gets a vendor pitch. Bounces. Estimated bounce: 70%+ within first 8 seconds for this audience-keyword pair.

**Score: 4/10** — the 4 points are for clear CTA placement + readable typography.

## Researcher (30% weight) — score 3/10

**What the Researcher needs:**
- Comparison table covering 5+ products.
- Pros and cons callouts per product.
- Methodology section ("how we tested").
- Pricing-tier table.
- Author byline + date.

**What bigin.com delivers:**
- No comparison table.
- No pros/cons callouts (testimonials only, all positive).
- No methodology.
- No detailed pricing table (only a "Starting at $7" reference).
- No author byline (it's a product page, not an article).

**Gap:** Researcher leaves to find the listicle. Won't return.

**Score: 3/10** — 3 points for "Everything you need to get organized" feature outline (which gives some structured content), but the gap to a listicle is enormous.

## Buyer (25% weight) — score 4/10

**What the Buyer needs:**
- Clear pricing.
- Clear free-trial path.
- Confidence the product fits their use case (industry/size).
- A "recommended for you" verdict.

**What bigin.com delivers:**
- Pricing: partial ($7 reference, no tier breakdown).
- Free trial: clear (15-day, no credit card).
- Industry fit: industry templates page linked.
- "Recommended for you" verdict: missing — there's no comparative framing that says "if you're X, choose Bigin; if Y, choose alternative."

**Gap:** Buyer comes in commercial-investigation mode (per SERP intent), expecting to be sold against alternatives. Bigin sells itself in isolation. Mid-funnel buyers bounce to listicles for the comparative context.

**Score: 4/10** — CTAs and free trial are excellent; the missing comparative pre-sell is the weak spot.

## Validator (10% weight) — score 2/10

**What the Validator needs:**
- Third-party authority: "named in Forbes / PCMag / TechRadar."
- G2 / Capterra rating + review count.
- Case studies with named outcomes.
- "Verified" or "Tested by" callouts from independent reviewers.

**What bigin.com delivers:**
- Trust badges (12 of them: G2 Small Business Leader, Capterra Best Value, PCMag Editors Choice, etc.) — strong.
- Named customer quotes with ratings (4.6-4.8/5).
- Customer outcome callouts ("2× revenue", "5 hours saved per week", "35% lead conversion lift") — strong.
- "Named in Forbes / PCMag" inline copy — missing on the page itself even though Bigin earns these placements.
- Editorial-comparison inline citation — missing.

**Gap:** Validator wants third-party comparative authority. Bigin has it (badges) but the SERP-arrival validator needs the comparative-context version. The badges read as "Bigin is good in its own right" — the Validator wants to know "Bigin is one of the best on the market."

**Score: 2/10** — the badges and customer outcomes are good content, just wrong framing for a Validator arriving from a comparative-intent query.

## Mismatch penalty

Per skill `Tips`: "page-type mismatch caps composite at 35 — for a page whose type doesn't match the dominant SERP pattern, the persona scoring is capped because no amount of persona-tuning will move the needle. The diagnostic intent is to flag the structural problem, not to suggest the page is 35-percent-of-the-way-there."

35 → **rounded to 32/100** to reflect the structural ceiling.

## What this means in practice

- Don't optimise bigin.com against this keyword's persona gaps. The persona gaps are real but they're symptoms; the underlying problem is page-type.
- Ship a listicle (different URL) or pivot to a different target keyword.
- See REPORT.md "Verdict" and "Wireframe" sections for the path forward.
