# Linear vs Jira: 2026 Comparison

> Snapshot dated 2026-05-14 · US database · Target keyword "linear vs jira" · Numbers will drift — re-run the skill for current data.

## TL;DR

Choose **Linear** if you're an engineering or product team that values speed, opinionated workflows, and an AI-first interface. The free tier gets you 2 teams + 250 issues, paid starts at $10/user/mo, and the product is designed around the actual practices of high-velocity product teams.

Choose **Jira** if you're a larger org that needs deep customisation, runs multiple departments on the same tracker, or needs the Atlassian ecosystem (Confluence, Bitbucket, Compass). Jira has a free tier up to 10 users, paid starts at $7.53/user/mo, and connects to 3,000+ apps including the Atlassian MCP for AI tool stacks.

**The honest split:** Linear wins on day-one velocity for ≤500-person product orgs. Jira wins on cross-functional breadth and enterprise customisation. The 50–500 employee SaaS company on the fence: try Linear first — migration cost off Linear is lower if you outgrow it than migration cost off Jira if you find it too heavy.

## Pricing

- Business: $16/user/mo — unlimited teams, private teams, Triage Intelligence, automations, Linear Insights, Linear Asks, Zendesk/Intercom
- Enterprise: Custom — SAML/SCIM, advanced org modeling, priority support

**Jira (Atlassian Cloud):**
- Free: $0 — up to 10 users
- Standard: $7.53/user/mo (typical)
- Premium: $13.53/user/mo (typical)
- Enterprise: Custom

**Reading:** Per-seat at the entry paid tier, Jira is $2.47/user/mo cheaper than Linear, but Linear's free tier extends much further on seats (unlimited members vs 10), so for under-10-user teams Linear is effectively free indefinitely while Jira's 10-user free cap forces a paid upgrade.

## Features

**Linear's bet:** opinionated workflow + speed. Issues, projects, cycles (sprints), roadmaps, and a calm AI-native interface with Linear Agent for PRD drafting and PR shipping. Triage Intelligence auto-labels and routes incoming work. Linear Asks captures Slack conversations as issues. The product is built for engineering-led teams who don't want to spend weeks configuring workflow state machines.

**Jira's bet:** infinite customisation. Custom fields, custom issue types, custom workflows with no-code transitions, custom dashboards, custom JQL queries, custom apps via the Forge platform. Rovo AI sits across Jira/Confluence/Bitbucket for cross-product AI work. The Teamwork Graph links work to goals and teams; the 3,000+ integration directory covers nearly every B2B SaaS tool.

For a 30-engineer startup: Linear ships in a day. For a 3,000-person org with finance, legal, marketing, IT, and engineering all tracking work: Jira is the tool that absorbs all of them.

## Integrations

**Linear integrations sampled:** Slack, GitHub (deep — branch/PR sync), GitLab, Figma, Zendesk, Intercom, Salesforce, Microsoft Teams, Sentry, Cron, Discord, Loom, Notion (via embed), and the Linear MCP for direct AI-tool access (Cursor, Claude Code, etc.).

**Jira integrations sampled:** Slack, GitHub, GitLab, Bitbucket (Atlassian-native), Confluence (Atlassian-native), Compass (Atlassian-native), Figma, Google Docs, Microsoft Teams, ServiceNow, Salesforce, Notion, Linear (yes — there's a Jira→Linear migration path), Zendesk, Intercom, Datadog, Splunk, and the Atlassian MCP.

**Reading:** Jira's 3,000+ count includes the long tail of niche connectors; Linear's ~100 covers the high-frequency stack but skips most enterprise-IT software (no Workday, no SAP, no Oracle, no Active Directory beyond SSO). If your stack is "every SaaS we use," Jira wins. If your stack is "GitHub + Slack + Figma + Cron + Notion," Linear's integration set is denser.

## Support

**Linear:** Email support on paid tiers. Business adds Zendesk/Intercom integration for support workflows. Enterprise adds priority support and account management.

**Jira:** Standard tier email support, Premium tier 24/7 support and 99.9% uptime SLA, Enterprise tier guaranteed response times and dedicated account team. Atlassian Community + 12k+ marketplace apps as long-tail support.

For under-100-user teams the support gap is mostly theoretical — both vendors respond in business hours. Above 1k users, Jira's 24/7 + SLA tier is a real differentiator.

## When to choose Linear

- You're an engineering-led product company ≤500 employees
- You want the tool to encode "how product orgs actually work" rather than be a blank canvas
- AI-native workflow (Linear Agent, Triage Intelligence) is a strategic priority
- You value the speed of the UI itself — Linear's keyboard-first interface is faster than Jira's at every common action
- You don't have a multi-department compliance/customisation requirement

## When to choose Jira

- You're a multi-department or large org (1k+ employees) where engineering is one team among many
- You need custom workflow state machines, custom issue types, custom fields at scale
- The Atlassian ecosystem (Confluence, Bitbucket, Compass) is part of your stack
- You need ITSM / Jira Service Management for IT tickets alongside engineering issues
- Compliance / audit trail requirements demand the depth of Jira's permissions model
- Your engineering org is comfortable with the configuration cost (or has a dedicated Jira admin)

## FAQ

**Is Linear easier to use than Jira?**
Yes, by design. Linear is opinionated about workflow (issues → cycles → projects → roadmap), so there's less to configure. Jira's strength — customisation — is also its learning-curve cost. Most teams say Linear took them under a day to set up and Jira took weeks.

**Can I migrate from Jira to Linear?**
Yes — Linear has a Jira import that maps issues, projects, and basic workflow state. Custom fields and custom issue types from Jira often need manual mapping. Linear publishes a migration guide; mid-size migrations (500–5,000 issues) typically take 1–3 days.

**Does Jira have an AI agent like Linear Agent?**
Yes — Rovo. Rovo is Atlassian's AI layer across Jira, Confluence, and Bitbucket. It's a separate add-on (additional per-user fee on most plans). Linear Agent is included in paid Linear tiers.

**Is Jira's free tier better than Linear's?**
Depends on team size. Jira's free tier supports up to 10 users — fine for tiny teams. Linear's free tier has unlimited members but caps issues at 250 and teams at 2. For an under-10-user team that doesn't have heavy issue volume yet, both are workable; for a fast-growing team, Linear's seat-unlimited free tier is the stronger gateway.

**Which is better for cross-functional teams (marketing + engineering)?**
Jira is the broader fit. Linear is primarily designed for product/engineering/design teams; marketing and ops users sometimes find the issue-cycle vocabulary unfamiliar. Jira's flexibility absorbs non-engineering teams better, especially with Jira Work Management for non-software work.
