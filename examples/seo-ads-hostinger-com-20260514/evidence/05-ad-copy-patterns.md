# 05 — Ad copy patterns

Status: SE Ranking MCP auth unavailable in this session for full ad-copy enumeration. Re-run /seo-ads with auth to populate clustering across the full paid-keyword set.

Patterns observed via Firecrawl scrape + Firecrawl SERP probes on 2026-05-14:

Pattern 1 — Price anchor in title or description:
- Hostinger: "From $2.99/mo" (pricing page)
- HoboHost: "Starting at Only $1" (homepage)
- GoDaddy: "Cheap Web Hosting | High Quality Hosting at a Low Price"
- Cluster type: numeric-anchor (HoboHost, Hostinger) or qualitative-anchor (GoDaddy)

Pattern 2 — Feature-bundle headline:
- Hostinger: "A Fast and Secure Platform for Your Website" (/web-hosting title)
- Namecheap: "Web Hosting - Your Path to the Perfect Hosting Plan"
- Cluster type: lifestyle-bundle, no specific feature called out

Pattern 3 — Brand-led:
- Hostinger og:title: "Hostinger pricing | Explore web hosting and all Hostinger plans"
- Brand-first formats relying on existing trust; common for established players.

Pattern 4 — Third-party trust signals (in SERP organic, likely mirrored in paid):
- CNET, Reddit review threads frequently rank organically; paid copy from established hosts can reference "as seen in CNET" / "X-star rated" to borrow third-party credibility.

Recommended ad-copy direction for Hostinger (assuming the goal is defending category leadership):
- Headline: price anchor at $2.99/mo to match Hostinger's existing pricing page promise.
- Description: pair price with one feature differentiator ("Free SSL + domain", "AI website builder included").
- Brand reinforcement: name-drop one third-party trust signal where Hostinger has positive coverage.
- CTA: "Start free trial" or "Get $2.99 hosting".
